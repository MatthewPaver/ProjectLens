# ProjectLens competitive landscape

**Generated:** 15 July 2026
**Depth:** Current public-product quick scan

## Landscape

The established project-intelligence market is already strong at private schedule ingestion, CPM diagnostics, activity-level forecasting, quantitative schedule risk and portfolio reporting. ProjectLens should not claim that an easier risk dashboard or an AI summary is novel.

| Product | Publicly evidenced strength | What ProjectLens should not copy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| nPlan | ML forecasting trained on a claimed 750,000 historical schedules, activity uncertainty, scenarios and portfolio deliverability | Proprietary activity-level prediction |
| SmartPM | Automated CPM schedule controls, update comparison, quality checks, delay analysis and what-if modelling | Construction schedule diagnostics |
| Nodes & Links | Portfolio health, schedule change analysis, AI QSRA, reports and natural-language access | “Ask your schedule” as the main proposition |
| InEight | Integrated scope, cost, schedule, change, field execution and historical knowledge | Full enterprise controls platform |

## Defensible gap

ProjectLens now has two connected but distinct wedges.

The public-evidence wedge is an **open, evidence-linked longitudinal delivery-confidence product**:

1. It starts with official public project disclosures, not a synthetic showcase.
2. It shows how ratings, end dates, annual variance and explanations changed across releases.
3. It preserves contradictions, such as an improved rating alongside a later end date.
4. It retrieves comparable projects that later improved and explains the similarity basis.
5. Every signal links to the original publication.
6. The attention score is fully specified and is never described as failure probability.

The operational wedge is a **lightweight assurance layer before the recurring management decision meeting**:

1. It does not replace P6, Excel, Word, PowerPoint, Jira or an enterprise controls platform.
2. It reconciles the evidence already exported from those systems.
3. It starts with the board questions: what changed, what conflicts, what is missing and what can be decided?
4. It retains the exact source and evidence version behind every finding.
5. It preserves the named human authority and follows approval conditions into the next reporting cycle.
6. Language-model use remains bounded to structuring or wording; code owns calculations and validation.

This is narrower than enterprise project controls and more operational than a generic AI summary.

## Positioning

> Project Evidence Desk tells a project board what the pack can prove, what still conflicts and which decisions need evidence—without replacing the systems the team already uses.

## Market opportunity

Enterprise tools optimise delivery inside organisations with access to detailed schedules. Public reporting portals usually provide annual snapshots. Generic assistants can summarise a supplied document but do not own cross-file reconciliation, deterministic controls, evidence versions, approval authority or follow-through.

ProjectLens occupies two related spaces:

- longitudinal, source-linked portfolio intelligence that is understandable without specialist scheduling software;
- a project-board assurance layer across fragmented exports, with a decision and condition trail.

This remains a market hypothesis, not validated demand. The strongest validation question is whether programme controls leads, PMO heads and change-authority chairs will provide a recent pack and spend 30 minutes comparing the output with their current pre-board process.

## Initial commercial package

| Package | User | Outcome | Evidence required |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Board-readiness pilot | Programme controls lead or PMO head | One board pack reconciled before the meeting | Two packs, RAID, actions/decisions, schedule export |
| Change-decision pilot | Change authority chair | One change assessed, decided and followed through | Current/previous schedule, narrative, decision authority |
| Public portfolio briefing | Adviser, researcher or supplier | Source-linked portfolio and project brief | Official GMPP releases |

Do not set SaaS pricing until five workflow interviews and two evidence-backed pilots establish frequency, time saved, acceptable data handling and the buyer.

## Validation gates

1. Five interviews with people who prepare or chair a project board.
2. Three anonymised real pack walkthroughs.
3. At least two users identify a material issue they would otherwise have found in the meeting.
4. One user returns with the next reporting period without prompting.
5. Security, retention, connector and audit requirements are documented before any production upload.

## Honest boundaries

- Annual GMPP data cannot support activity-level critical-path forecasting.
- Delivery Confidence Assessments are point-in-time judgements, not final outcomes.
- Narrative theme matching organises evidence but does not prove causation.
- Comparable cases prompt investigation and do not prescribe interventions.
- A production internal version would require secure schedule connectors, permissions, audit logs and organisation-specific validation.
