# Project schedule-risk market scan

**Generated:** 2026-07-14  
**Depth:** Quick scan of public product material

## Landscape

The established market is strong at schedule diagnostics, risk registers, Monte Carlo analysis and specialist recovery modelling. Newer browser products are closing the usability gap with uploads, AI explanations, automated reports and executive summaries. A product positioned only as “schedule risk, but easier” would therefore be weakly differentiated.

| Product | Strongest public signal | Implication for ProjectLens |
|---|---|---|
| Oracle Primavera Cloud | Integrated schedules, risks, cost and quantitative analysis | Do not attempt to replicate an enterprise planning suite |
| Deltek Acumen | Deep diagnostics, forensics and recovery modelling | Keep the model transparent and the workflow focused |
| Safran Risk | Specialist pre/post mitigation quantitative analysis | Scenario comparison alone is not a gap |
| Kazinex | Modern browser workflow, reporting and AI assistance | Upload and explanation are expected features |
| TeamVase | Executive explanations, recommendations and portfolio views | The original concept already has a direct competitor |

## Defensible gap

ProjectLens will focus on the **decision-to-outcome loop**:

1. Identify the evidence-backed risk driver.
2. Compare a small number of operational interventions on recovery, confidence, effort and side effects.
3. Record which intervention was selected, by whom, with an owner and review date.
4. On the next schedule update, compare promised recovery with observed recovery.
5. Preserve the decision history so teams can learn which interventions work in their delivery environment.

This is narrower than a full project-controls platform but more useful in a weekly or monthly controls meeting than another static risk dashboard.

## Positioning

> ProjectLens turns schedule analysis into accountable recovery decisions, then checks whether those decisions worked.

## Boundaries

- ProjectLens is a portfolio-grade decision-support demonstrator, not a replacement for P6, Acumen or Safran.
- Public data and results are synthetic and clearly labelled.
- Recommendations remain reviewable rules with visible evidence, not autonomous instructions.
- Production adoption would require authentication, permissions, secure storage, connectors and organisational calibration.
